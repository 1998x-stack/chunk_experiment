Finite size scaling theory for percolation with multiple giant clusters

Yong Zhu    Xiaosong Chen State Key Laboratory of Theoretical Physics, Institute of Theoretical Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, P.O. Box 2735, Beijing 100190, China
Abstract

A approach of finite size scaling theory for discontinous percolation with multiple giant clusters is developed in this paper. The percolation in generalized Bohman-Frieze-Wormald (BFW) model has already been proved to be discontinuous phase transition. In the evolution process, the size of largest cluster s1subscript𝑠1s_{1} increases in a stairscase way and its fluctuation shows a series of peaks corresponding to the jumps of s1subscript𝑠1s_{1} from one stair to another. Several largest jumps of the size of largest cluster from single edge are studied by extensive Monte Carlo simulation. Δ¯k​(N)subscript¯Δ𝑘𝑁\overline{\Delta}_{k}(N) which is the mean of the k𝑘kth largest jump of largest cluster, r¯k​(N)subscript¯𝑟𝑘𝑁\overline{r}_{k}(N) which is the corresponding averaged edge density, σΔ,k​(N)subscript𝜎Δ𝑘𝑁\sigma_{\Delta,k}(N) which is the standard deviation of ΔksubscriptΔ𝑘\Delta_{k} and σr,k​(N)subscript𝜎𝑟𝑘𝑁\sigma_{r,k}(N) which is the standard deviation of rksubscript𝑟𝑘r_{k} are analyzed. Rich power law behaviours are found for r¯k​(N)subscript¯𝑟𝑘𝑁\overline{r}_{k}(N), σΔ,k​(N)subscript𝜎Δ𝑘𝑁\sigma_{\Delta,k}(N) and σr,k​(N)subscript𝜎𝑟𝑘𝑁\sigma_{r,k}(N) with critical exponents denoted as 1/ν11subscript𝜈11/\nu_{1}, (β/ν)2subscript𝛽𝜈2(\beta/\nu)_{2} and 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2}. Unlike continuous percolation where the exact critical thresholds and critical exponent 1/ν11subscript𝜈11/\nu_{1} are used for finite size scaling, the size-dependent pseudo critical thresholds r¯k​(N)subscript¯𝑟𝑘𝑁\overline{r}_{k}(N) and 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} works for the data collapse of the curves of largest cluster and its fluctuation in discontinuous percolation in BFW model. Further, data collapse can be obtained part by part. That is, s1​(r,N)subscript𝑠1𝑟𝑁s_{1}(r,N) can be collapsed for each jump from one stair to another and its fluctuation can be collapsed around each peak with the corresponding r¯k​(N)subscript¯𝑟𝑘𝑁\overline{r}_{k}(N) and 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2}.

I Introduction

Percolation transition represents the emergence of cluster large enough to compare with the system size as the density of occupied bonds or sites incerases. In classical percolation, bonds or sites are selected and occupied randomly and independently. It is continuous and accompanied by universal critical exponents depending only on dimensionality of the system and the number of components of the order parameter in both regular lattice of different dimensions and complex networks with different topologies. Instead of occupation bonds or sites randomly, Achilioptas process proposed in Ref.Achlioptas refers to adding one edge or site of some candidates on some certain conditions at each step and the percolation under product rule was claimed to be discontinuous in this seminal papaer. However, percolation transition under AP is identified to be continuous by both rigorous prove Riordan11 and analysis of MC simulation data Costa10 ; Liu11 ; Grassberger11 ; Fan12 . It is the different critical exponents that makes the explosive percolation unusual.

Recently, a so called BFW model introduced by Bohman, Frieze and Wormald Bohman has attrated much attention Chenw11 ; Chenw12 ; schrenk12 ; Zhangy12 ; Chenw13a ; Chenw13b ; Zhangrq13 ; Chenw14 . The BFW model starts N isolated nodes and a parameter k𝑘k which indicates the largest cluster allowed and is initialized as 2. At each step one edge is selected uniformly at random and it would accepted only if the size of the component formed from it is not larger than k𝑘k. Otherwise k𝑘k would be augmented to k+1𝑘1k+1 and the edge would be rejudged if the fraction of accepted edges is smaller than a function g​(k)=1/2+(2​k)−1/2𝑔𝑘12superscript2𝑘12g(k)=1/2+(2k)^{-1/2} while a new edge would sampled randomly if the fraction of accepted edges is not. Asymptotically, half of the sampled edges would be accepted. This model is introduced to avoid the appearance of giant component.

Chen and D’Souza Chenw11 generalized BFW model by taking the asymptotic fraction of accepted edges as a parameter α𝛼\alpha which is 0.5 in the original BFW model. The generalized BFW model exhibits percoaltion transition with the simultaneous emergence of multiple giant clusters which are stable and the transition is discontinuous for all α𝛼\alpha belongs to(0,0.97]. As α𝛼\alpha decreases, the number of giant clusters would increase, the sizes of giant clusters would decrease and the critical threshold of percolation transition would be delayed. When only one giant cluster exists and stops growing, a second giant component emerges in a continuous percolaiton transition whose critical points and exponents are dependent on α𝛼\alpha. Through the analysis of BFW model generalized by taking g​(k)=0.5+(2​k)β𝑔𝑘0.5superscript2𝑘𝛽g(k)=0.5+(2k)^{\beta}, they found the underlying mechanism for discontinuous transition is growth by overtaking Chenw12 . BFW model on square and simple-cubic lattices was investigated by Schrenk and numerical evidence for strongly discontinuous transitions was found schrenk12 . Zhang built a relation between α𝛼\alpha and the number of giant clusters Zhangy12 . It indicated there would be only one giant cluster if α>0.52𝛼0.52\alpha>0.52. In Ref.Chenw13b , the authors classified the BFM into two α𝛼\alpha regimes, one is the stable regime of a unique discontinuous transition where one or more giant components emerge and coexist throughout the supercritical regime while the other one is the unstable regime of multiple discontinuous transitions where multiple giant components emerge but the two smallest ones merge at a well-defined transition point in the supercritical regime.

Finite size scaling theory for continuous percolation transitions has been well established and has been used to characterize critical behavior of phase transition by critical threshold and critical exponentsLiu11 ; Fan12 ; Privman84 ; Privman90 . For example, the reduced size of largest cluster of percolation on regular lattice follows a scaling form near the critical probability pcsubscript𝑝𝑐p_{c}

VI Summary

We have investigated discontinuous percolation in generalized BFW model and developed a approach of finite size scaling for it. We analysis the behaviors of the k𝑘kth largest jump, induced by single edge, of the size of largest cluster with k𝑘k up to 3. Similar to continuous percolation, rich power laws are found. The mean k𝑘kth largest jump Δk​(N)subscriptΔ𝑘𝑁\Delta_{k}(N) converges to a positive constant indicating the discontinuity of percolation. The corresponding averaged edge densities rk​(N)subscript𝑟𝑘𝑁r_{k}(N) always converge to the real critical thresholds in power law with exponents 1/ν11subscript𝜈11/\nu_{1} which is 0.5 except when α=0.1𝛼0.1\alpha=0.1. The standard deviation of Δk​(N)subscriptΔ𝑘𝑁\Delta_{k}(N) converges to zero in power law with exponent (β/ν)2subscript𝛽𝜈2(\beta/\nu)_{2} which differs slightly for different α𝛼\alpha. The standard deviation of rk​(N)subscript𝑟𝑘𝑁r_{k}(N) converges to zero in power law with exponent 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} which is not always the same for the three largest jump while it is always the same for continuous percolation. 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} converges to 0.5 as α𝛼\alpha increases.

For continuous percolation, the real critical thresholds and critical exponents 1/ν11subscript𝜈11/\nu_{1} take the responsibility of finite size scaling with the scaling variable (r−rc)​L1/ν1𝑟subscript𝑟𝑐superscript𝐿1subscript𝜈1(r-r_{c})L^{1/\nu_{1}}. However, for discontinuous percolation in BFW model, data collapse can be achieved with the size-dependent pseudo critical thresholds rk​(N)subscript𝑟𝑘𝑁r_{k}(N) and critcal exponents 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} from the corresponding standard deviation of rk​(N)subscript𝑟𝑘𝑁r_{k}(N). When α=0.8,0.9𝛼0.80.9\alpha=0.8,0.9, the critical exponents 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} from three largest jumps are approximately the same and data collapse can be done once and for all. For the cases with different 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} from three largest jumps, data collapse can be attained piecewise. The part of s1subscript𝑠1s_{1} where the largest jump takes place and the part of χ/N𝜒𝑁\chi/N around the highest peak can be collapsed together with r1​(N)subscript𝑟1𝑁r_{1}(N) and 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} from σr1​(N)superscriptsubscript𝜎𝑟1𝑁\sigma_{r}^{1}(N), the part of s1subscript𝑠1s_{1} where the second largest jump takes place and the part of χ/N𝜒𝑁\chi/N around the second highest peak can be collapsed together with r2​(N)subscript𝑟2𝑁r_{2}(N) and 1/ν21subscript𝜈21/\nu_{2} from σr2​(N)superscriptsubscript𝜎𝑟2𝑁\sigma_{r}^{2}(N), and so on.
