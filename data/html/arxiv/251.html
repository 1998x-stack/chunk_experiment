1. Introduction

One of the oldest problems in algebra is to decide whether an equation over a given algebraic structure has a solution. In the last decades this problem has received increasing attention from a computational complexity point of view; in particular for finite algebras the aim is to identify conditions that either imply tractability or hardness of the corresponding equation solvability problem. Many results are known for finite groups and rings, of which several are based on commutator theory. For rings a complexity dichotomy holds: InÂ [3] it was shown that the equation solvability problem over non-nilpotent rings is NP-complete, byÂ [8] the problem is in P for nilpotent rings.

Nilpotency is also a source of tractability in the group case: It was proven inÂ [7] (and reproven inÂ [8]) that the equation solvability over nilpotent groups is in P. ByÂ [12] non-solvable groups induce NP-complete problems. Furthermore it was shown in [14] that every solvable, non-nilpotent group has a polynomial extension, whose equation solvability problem is NP-complete. However it is still open whether a complexity dichotomy like over rings holds. In particular nilpotency does not demark the border between problems in P and NP-complete: ByÂ [15] the equation solvability over the non-nilpotent group A4subscriptğ´4A_{4} is in P but its extension by the commutator [â‹…,â‹…]â‹…â‹…[\cdot,\cdot] has an NP-complete equation solvability problem. More general, meta-abelian groupsÂ [10] and semipattern groupsÂ [4] induce equation solvability problems that are in P, while not necessarily being nilpotent.

Congruence permutable varieties generalize both the varieties of groups and rings and are well-studied in the context of commutator theory. It is hence natural to ask, whether the above dichotomy results can be generalized to all congruence permutable varieties. It was already observed inÂ [1] that the identity checking problem for supernilpotent such algebras is in P. Also our results indicate that not nilpotency, but supernilpotency is the right notion to work with: In SectionÂ 2 we show that the equation solvability problems over finite supernilpotent algebras with Malâ€™cev term are in P. As a corollary of our proof we obtain a new characterization of supernilpotent algebras in congruence permutable varieties. In SectionÂ 3 we give examples of nilpotent, but not supernilpotent algebras (of infinite type) that have a Malâ€™cev term and induce coNP-complete identity checking problems and NP-complete equation solvability problems.
