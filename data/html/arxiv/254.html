Abstract

Under the approximate chiral symmetry restoration, quark interactions with topological gluon fields in quantum chromodynamics can induce chirality imbalance and parity violation in local domains. An electric charge separation (cs) could be generated along the direction of a strong magnetic field (ùêÅùêÅ{\bf B}), a phenomenon called the chiral magnetic effect (cme). cs¬†measurements by azimuthal correlators are contaminated by major backgrounds from elliptic flow anisotropy (v2subscriptùë£2v_{2}). We show, by using the density functional theory calculations of the proton and neutron distributions, that these expectations may not hold as originally anticipated because the two systems may have sizable differences in eccentricity and v2subscriptùë£2v_{2}.

Nuclear densities. Because of the different numbers of protons‚Äìwhich suffer from Coulomb repulsion‚Äìand neutrons, the structures of the 4496subscriptsuperscriptabsent9644{}^{96}_{44}Ru¬†and 4096subscriptsuperscriptabsent9640{}^{96}_{40}Zr¬†nuclei must not be identical. In summary, topological charge fluctuations are a fundamental property of QCD, which could lead to the chiral magnetic effect (cme) and charge separation (cs) in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Experimental cs¬†measurements have suffered from major backgrounds from resonance decays coupled with elliptic flow anisotropy (v2subscriptùë£2v_{2}). To reduce background effects, isobaric 4496subscriptsuperscriptabsent9644{}^{96}_{44}Ru+4496subscriptsuperscriptabsent9644{}^{96}_{44}Ru¬†and 4096subscriptsuperscriptabsent9640{}^{96}_{40}Zr+4096subscriptsuperscriptabsent9640{}^{96}_{40}Zr¬†collisions have been proposed where the v2subscriptùë£2v_{2}-induced backgrounds are expected to be similar while the cme-induced signals to be different. In this Letter, the proton and neutron density distributions of 4496subscriptsuperscriptabsent9644{}^{96}_{44}Ru¬†and 4096subscriptsuperscriptabsent9640{}^{96}_{40}Zr¬†are calculated using the energy density functional theory (dft). They are then implemented in the Monte Carlo Glauber (mcg) model to calculate the eccentricities (œµ2subscriptitalic-œµ2\epsilon_{2}) and magnetic fields (ùêÅùêÅ{\bf B}); the dft¬†densities are implemented in A Multi-Phase Transport (ampt) model to simulate the v2subscriptùë£2v_{2}. It is found that those nuclear densities, together with the Woods-Saxon (ws) densities, yield wide ranges of differences in Bsq¬Ø¬Øsubscriptùêµsq\overline{B_{\rm sq}} with respect to the participant plane (pp) and the reaction plane (rp). It is further found that those nuclear densities introduce, in contrast to ws, comparable differences in œµ2‚Äã{œàRP}subscriptitalic-œµ2subscriptùúìRP\epsilon_{2}\{\psi_{{}_{\rm RP}}\} (v2‚Äã{œàRP}subscriptùë£2subscriptùúìRPv_{2}\{\psi_{{}_{\rm RP}}\}) and Bsq¬Ø‚Äã{œàRP}¬ØsubscriptùêµsqsubscriptùúìRP\overline{B_{\rm sq}}\{\psi_{{}_{\rm RP}}\} with respect to the reaction plane (rp), diminishing the premise of isobaric collisions to help identify the cme. With respect to the participant plane (pp), the œµ2‚Äã{œàPP}subscriptitalic-œµ2subscriptùúìPP\epsilon_{2}\{\psi_{{}_{\rm PP}}\} (v2‚Äã{œàEP}subscriptùë£2subscriptùúìEPv_{2}\{\psi_{{}_{\rm EP}}\}) difference can still be sizable, as large as ‚àº3similar-toabsent3\sim 3%, possibly weakening the power of isobaric collisions for the cme¬†search.

Since the dft¬†calculation of the matter radius is smaller for Ru and Zr, the produced particle multiplicity distribution would have a higher tail in RuRu¬†than in ZrZr, as predicted by ampt. This can be checked against results using density distributions of larger 4496subscriptsuperscriptabsent9644{}^{96}_{44}Ru¬†than 4096subscriptsuperscriptabsent9640{}^{96}_{40}Zr¬†radius, such as ws¬†densities using charge radii in place of matter radii. We further predict, using the dft¬†calculated density distributions, that the v2subscriptùë£2v_{2} difference between RuRu¬†and ZrZr¬†with respect to the rp¬†is larger than that with respect to the pp¬†by an absolute 8%, insensitive to uncertainties in the nuclear deformities, while it is practically zero for ws. This can be experimentally tested by the upcoming isobaric collisions; a confirmation would be a good indication of the validity of the density distributions calculated here for the Ru and Zr nuclei. Our study would then be a valuable guidance to the experimental isobaric collision program at rhic.
