The cup product of Brooks quasimorphisms

Michelle Bucher University of Geneva, 1211 Geneva 4, Switzerland michelle.bucher-karlsson@unige.ch  and  Nicolas Monod EPFL, SB-MATH-EGG, 1015 Lausanne, Switzerland nicolas.monod@epfl.ch
Abstract.

We prove the vanishing of the cup product of the bounded cohomology classes associated to any two Brooks quasimorphisms on the free group. This is a consequence of the vanishing of the square of a universal class for tree automorphism groups.

1. Introduction

Although bounded cohomology found a great variety of applications, it remains so mysterious that even for a (non-abelian) free group F𝐹F of finite rank, we do not know much about it.

More precisely, beyond the trivial case of Hb1​(F,𝐑)=0superscriptsubscriptHb1𝐹𝐑0\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{1}(F,\mathbf{R})=0, it is known that both Hb2​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb2𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{2}(F,\mathbf{R}) and Hb3​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb3𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{3}(F,\mathbf{R}) are infinite-dimensional. However, Hbn≥4​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb𝑛4𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{n\geq 4}(F,\mathbf{R}) remains completely unknown; in particular, we do not know whether Hb4​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb4𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{4}(F,\mathbf{R}) vanishes or not.

The first infinite family of non-trivial classes in Hb2​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb2𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{2}(F,\mathbf{R}) are provided by Brooks quasimorphisms [1] (anticipated by Johnson [7, 2.8] and Rhemtulla [12]); we recall their definition. Pick any reduced word w𝑤w in a choice of free generators for F𝐹F and consider the counting function fw:F→𝐑:subscript𝑓𝑤→𝐹𝐑f_{w}\colon F\rightarrow\mathbf{R} defined on g∈F𝑔𝐹g\in F by

If w𝑤w is reduced to one letter (or trivial), then fwsubscript𝑓𝑤f_{w} is a homomorphism. In all other cases, fwsubscript𝑓𝑤f_{w} is a quasimorphism and defines a non-trivial class βw∈Hb2​(F,𝐑)subscript𝛽𝑤superscriptsubscriptHb2𝐹𝐑\beta_{w}\in\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{2}(F,\mathbf{R}) unless w𝑤w is conjugated to a power of a letter. The space spanned by all these βwsubscript𝛽𝑤\beta_{w} is infinite-dimensional [1][8] and is dense in Hb2​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb2𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{2}(F,\mathbf{R}) for a suitable topology of pointwise convergence [4, 5.7]. The aim of this note is to show that the cup product of any two elements in this dense sub-space vanishes in Hb4​(F,𝐑)superscriptsubscriptHb4𝐹𝐑\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}^{4}(F,\mathbf{R}).
