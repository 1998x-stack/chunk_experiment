I Introduction

Percolation transition represents the emergence of cluster large enough to compare with the system size as the density of occupied bonds or sites incerases. In classical percolation, bonds or sites are selected and occupied randomly and independently. It is continuous and accompanied by universal critical exponents depending only on dimensionality of the system and the number of components of the order parameter in both regular lattice of different dimensions and complex networks with different topologies. Instead of occupation bonds or sites randomly, Achilioptas process proposed in Ref.Achlioptas refers to adding one edge or site of some candidates on some certain conditions at each step and the percolation under product rule was claimed to be discontinuous in this seminal papaer. However, percolation transition under AP is identified to be continuous by both rigorous proveÂ Riordan11 and analysis of MC simulation dataÂ Costa10 ; Liu11 ; Grassberger11 ; Fan12 . It is the different critical exponents that makes the explosive percolation unusual.

Recently, a so called BFW model introduced by Bohman, Frieze and WormaldÂ Bohman has attrated much attentionÂ Chenw11 ; Chenw12 ; schrenk12 ; Zhangy12 ; Chenw13a ; Chenw13b ; Zhangrq13 ; Chenw14 . The BFW model starts N isolated nodes and a parameter kğ‘˜k which indicates the largest cluster allowed and is initialized as 2. At each step one edge is selected uniformly at random and it would accepted only if the size of the component formed from it is not larger than kğ‘˜k.
