Duality-mediated critical amplitude ratios for the (2+1)21(2+1)-dimensional S=1𝑆1S=1 X​Y𝑋𝑌XY model

Yoshihiro Nishiyama
(Received: date / Revised version: date)
Abstract

The phase transition for the (2+1)21(2+1)-dimensional spin-S=1𝑆1S=1 X​Y𝑋𝑌XY model was investigated numerically. Because of the boson-vortex duality, the spin stiffness ρssubscript𝜌𝑠\rho_{s} in the ordered phase and the vortex-condensate stiffness ρvsubscript𝜌𝑣\rho_{v} in the disordered phase should have a close relationship. We employed the exact diagonalization method, which yields the excitation gap directly. As a result, we estimate the amplitude ratios ρs,v/Δsubscript𝜌𝑠𝑣Δ\rho_{s,v}/\Delta (ΔΔ\Delta: Mott insulator gap) by means of the scaling analyses for the finite-size cluster with N≤22𝑁22N\leq 22 spins. The ratio ρs/ρvsubscript𝜌𝑠subscript𝜌𝑣\rho_{s}/\rho_{v} admits a quantitative measure of deviation from selfduality.

1 Introduction

The (2+1)21(2+1)-dimensional boson system undergoes the superfluid-insulator transition, which belongs to the three-dimensional X​Y𝑋𝑌XY universality class. The system possesses the boson-vortex duality Stone78 ; Fisher89 ; Wen90 , which brings about rich characters as to the critical phenomenon. The superfluid (ordered) and insulator (disordered) phases are characterized by the spin- and vortex-condensate-stiffness constants ρs,vsubscript𝜌𝑠𝑣\rho_{s,v}, respectively. The critical amplitude ratio ρs/ρvsubscript𝜌𝑠subscript𝜌𝑣\rho_{s}/\rho_{v} admits a “quantitative measure” Gazit14 of deviation from selfduality. Note that each stiffness constant ρs,vsubscript𝜌𝑠𝑣\rho_{s,v} yields the Drude weight for the corresponding AC conductivity Swanson14 , and it may be accessible Gazit14 experimentally Corson99 ; Crane07 ; Sherson10 . In this sense, the duality-mediated amplitude ratio is not a mere theoretical concept.

In Fig. 1, we present a schematic drawing for the stiffness constants (order parameters) ρs,vsubscript𝜌𝑠𝑣\rho_{s,v}, which develop in the superfluid (δ​JN​N>0𝛿subscript𝐽𝑁𝑁0\delta J_{NN}>0) and insulator (δ​JN​N<0𝛿subscript𝐽𝑁𝑁0\delta J_{NN}<0) phases, respectively. In the respective phases, there open the Higgs-mass mHsubscript𝑚𝐻m_{H} and the Mott-insulator ΔΔ\Delta gaps above the ground state; in the terminology of the quantum spin model, the former (latter) corresponds to the longitudinal mode of the magnetic moment (paramagnetic massive excitation). (On the one hand, the Goldstone excitation corresponds to the transverse mode of the magnetic moment.) A key ingredient is that these quantities possess the identical scaling dimension, and the amplitude ratios among them make sense.

Recently, the duality-mediated amplitude ratios ρs,v/Δsubscript𝜌𝑠𝑣Δ\rho_{s,v}/\Delta came under theoretical investigation by means of the Monte Carlo Gazit14 ; Gazit13 and renormalization-group Rose17 ; Rose15 ; Ranson13 methods. In prior to these studies, the mass-gap amplitude ratio mH/Δsubscript𝑚𝐻Δm_{H}/\Delta has been investigated rather extensively Gazit13 ; Gazit13a ; Chen13 ; Nishiyama15 ; Rancon14 ; Katan15 . As a matter of fact, for the two-dimensional ultra cold atom, these mass gaps were observed in proximity to the superfluid-insulator transition Endres12 ; see Ref. Pekker15 for a review.

The aim of this paper is to investigate these amplitude ratios for the (2+1)21(2+1)-dimensional S=1𝑆1S=1 X​Y𝑋𝑌XY model (1) by means of the exact diagonalization method. This method allows us to calculate the energy gaps such as mHsubscript𝑚𝐻m_{H} and ΔΔ\Delta without resorting to the inverse Laplace transformation Gazit13 . Moreover, the ground-state resolvent (response function) such as the second term of Eq. (5) is evaluated by means of the continued-fraction-expansion method Gagliano87 ; the continued-fraction-expansion method is essentially the same as the Lanczos tri-diagonalization algorithm, and computationally less demanding. Otherwise, the finite-temperature effect (anisotropy between the imaginary-time and real-space system sizes) has to be considered carefully Gazit14s .

We implemented the screw-boundary condition Novotny90 , which enables us to treat a variety of system sizes N=10,12,…,22𝑁1012…22N=10,12,\dots,22; the simulation algorithm is presented in Ref. Nishiyama08 . Because the N𝑁N spins constitute a rectangular cluster, the linear dimension of the cluster is given by L=N𝐿𝑁L=\sqrt{N}, which sets a fundamental length scale for the scaling analyses.
