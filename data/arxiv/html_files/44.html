1 Introduction

The (2+1)21(2+1)-dimensional boson system undergoes the superfluid-insulator transition, which belongs to the three-dimensional Xâ€‹Yğ‘‹ğ‘ŒXY universality class. The system possesses the boson-vortex duality Stone78 ; Fisher89 ; Wen90 , which brings about rich characters as to the critical phenomenon. The superfluid (ordered) and insulator (disordered) phases are characterized by the spin- and vortex-condensate-stiffness constants Ïs,vsubscriptğœŒğ‘ ğ‘£\rho_{s,v}, respectively. The critical amplitude ratio Ïs/ÏvsubscriptğœŒğ‘ subscriptğœŒğ‘£\rho_{s}/\rho_{v} admits a â€œquantitative measureâ€ Gazit14 of deviation from selfduality. Note that each stiffness constant Ïs,vsubscriptğœŒğ‘ ğ‘£\rho_{s,v} yields the Drude weight for the corresponding AC conductivity Swanson14 , and it may be accessible Gazit14 experimentally Corson99 ; Crane07 ; Sherson10 . In this sense, the duality-mediated amplitude ratio is not a mere theoretical concept.

In Fig. 1, we present a schematic drawing for the stiffness constants (order parameters) Ïs,vsubscriptğœŒğ‘ ğ‘£\rho_{s,v}, which develop in the superfluid (Î´â€‹JNâ€‹N>0ğ›¿subscriptğ½ğ‘ğ‘0\delta J_{NN}>0) and insulator (Î´â€‹JNâ€‹N<0ğ›¿subscriptğ½ğ‘ğ‘0\delta J_{NN}<0) phases, respectively. In the respective phases, there open the Higgs-mass mHsubscriptğ‘šğ»m_{H} and the Mott-insulator Î”Î”\Delta gaps above the ground state; in the terminology of the quantum spin model, the former (latter) corresponds to the longitudinal mode of the magnetic moment (paramagnetic massive excitation). (On the one hand, the Goldstone excitation corresponds to the transverse mode of the magnetic moment.) A key ingredient is that these quantities possess the identical scaling dimension, and the amplitude ratios among them make sense.

Recently, the duality-mediated amplitude ratios Ïs,v/Î”subscriptğœŒğ‘ ğ‘£Î”\rho_{s,v}/\Delta came under theoretical investigation by means of the Monte Carlo Gazit14 ; Gazit13 and renormalization-group Rose17 ; Rose15 ; Ranson13 methods. In prior to these studies, the mass-gap amplitude ratio mH/Î”subscriptğ‘šğ»Î”m_{H}/\Delta has been investigated rather extensively Gazit13 ; Gazit13a ; Chen13 ; Nishiyama15 ; Rancon14 ; Katan15 . As a matter of fact, for the two-dimensional ultra cold atom, these mass gaps were observed in proximity to the superfluid-insulator transition Endres12 ; see Ref. Pekker15 for a review.

The aim of this paper is to investigate these amplitude ratios for the (2+1)21(2+1)-dimensional S=1ğ‘†1S=1 Xâ€‹Yğ‘‹ğ‘ŒXY model (1) by means of the exact diagonalization method. This method allows us to calculate the energy gaps such as mHsubscriptğ‘šğ»m_{H} and Î”Î”\Delta without resorting to the inverse Laplace transformation Gazit13 . Moreover, the ground-state resolvent (response function) such as the second term of Eq. (5) is evaluated by means of the continued-fraction-expansion method Gagliano87 ; the continued-fraction-expansion method is essentially the same as the Lanczos tri-diagonalization algorithm, and computationally less demanding. Otherwise, the finite-temperature effect (anisotropy between the imaginary-time and real-space system sizes) has to be considered carefully Gazit14s .
