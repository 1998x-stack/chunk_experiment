Revealing the phase transition behaviors of k-core percolation in random networks

Yong Zhu    Xiaosong Chen State Key Laboratory of Theoretical Physics, Institute of Theoretical Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, P.O. Box 2735, Beijing 100190, China
(March 16, 2024)
Abstract

The k𝑘k-core percolation is a fundamental structural transition in complex networks. Through the analysis of the size jump behaviors of k𝑘k-core in the evolution process of networks, we confirm that k𝑘k-core percolation is continuous phase transition when k=1,2𝑘12k=1,2 while it is a hybrid first-order-second-order phase transition when k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3. 222-core percolation belongs to different universality class from that of 111-core (giant component) percolation. The discontinuity of k𝑘k-core percolation with k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3 can be concluded from largest size jump of k𝑘k-core which will not disappear in the thermodynamic limit while its continuous characteristic is reflected by second largest size jump which converges to zero in power law as N→∞→𝑁N\to\infty. Furthermore, along with the previously known exponent β=0.5𝛽0.5\beta=0.5, we obtain a set of exponents which are independent of k𝑘k when k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3 and also different from those critical exponents of 111-core and 222-core percolation.

I Introduction

The k𝑘k-core Bollobas84 of a network is its largest subgraph each of whose vertices has at least k nearest neighbors within this subgraph. k𝑘k-core (bootstrap) percolation which implies the emergence of the giant k𝑘k-core when edges are added randomly into the networks was first introduced on Bethe networks by J.Chalupa et al.Chalupa79

When k=1𝑘1k=1, 111-core percolation leads to well studied percolation of the giant component which is a continuous phase transition occurs at a critical connecting probability pcsubscript𝑝𝑐p_{c}. When p≥pc𝑝subscript𝑝𝑐p\geq p_{c}, the relative size of giant component satisfies s∝(p−pc)βproportional-to𝑠superscript𝑝subscript𝑝𝑐𝛽s\propto(p-p_{c})^{\beta} where β=1𝛽1\beta=1 according to the mean-field theory. The 222-core is similar but note identical to the bi-connected component of a network since a bi-connected component is surely a 222-core while the reverse is not. 222-core percolation is also continuous phase transition with the same critical point as percolation of giant component but the critical exponent β=2𝛽2\beta=2.

In contrast to k𝑘k-core percolation with k=1,2𝑘12k=1,2, when k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3, the k𝑘k-core appears suddenly at a transition point pcsubscript𝑝𝑐p_{c}. When p≥pc𝑝subscript𝑝𝑐p\geq p_{c}, the relative size of k𝑘k-core increases as

s​(p)−s​(pc)∝(p−pc)βproportional-to𝑠𝑝𝑠subscript𝑝𝑐superscript𝑝subscript𝑝𝑐𝛽s(p)-s(p_{c})\propto(p-p_{c})^{\beta} (1)

where β=1/2𝛽12\beta=1/2 and s​(pc)𝑠subscript𝑝𝑐s(p_{c}) is a nonzero constant which increases with k𝑘k. It indicates that the order parameter has a jump at p=pc𝑝subscript𝑝𝑐p=p_{c} from 0 to s​(pc)𝑠subscript𝑝𝑐s(p_{c}) as at an ordinary first-order phase transition as well as a singular behaviour as at a continuous phase transition Stauffer94 . Thus k𝑘k-core percolation with k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3 on Bethe networks is a hybrid phase transition.

This intriguing hybrid characteristic is also discovered for k𝑘k-core percolation in Erdös-Rényi (ER) networksPittel96 and configuration model with arbitrary degree distribution Dorogovtsev06a ; Dorogovtsev06b ; Goltsev06 , heterogeneous k𝑘k-core percolation Baxter11 , core percolation in directed networks where the in- and out-degree distributions differ Liuyy12 and a two parameter-dependent (K,K′)−limit-from𝐾superscript𝐾′(K,K^{{}^{\prime}})-protected core percolation Zhaojh13 .

It is worth mentioning that the hybrid nature of k𝑘k-core percolation expressed as Eq.(1) has not been revealed through simulation method yet. In this paper, we will use Monte Carlo simulation to investigate the k𝑘k-core in ER networks and illustrate its phase transition properties with the analysis of the size jump behaviors of k𝑘k-core in the evolution process of networks. The rest of this paper is organized as follows. In Sec.II, we introduce k𝑘k-core percolation in ER random networks and method to be used to analyze the simulation data. Our results of continuous k𝑘k-core percolation (k=1,2𝑘12k=1,2) and hybrid k𝑘k-core percolation (k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3) are presented in Sec.III and IV respectively. Finally a summarization is given in Sec.V.

II Model and Method

k𝑘k-core percolation in ER random networks was first studied by Pittel et al. through probabilistic analysis with transition point pcsubscript𝑝𝑐p_{c} and s​(pc)𝑠subscript𝑝𝑐s(p_{c}) obtained Pittel96 . The k𝑘k-core of a specific random network can be obtained in the following way. Remove all vertices of degree less than k𝑘k from the network. Some of the rest of the vertices may remain with less than k edges. Then remove these vertices, and so on, until no further removal is possible. It has been pointed out by Pittel et al. that, for ER random networks, the remaining vertices after pruning process, if there are, belong to a single k𝑘k-core Pittel96 . Accordingly, the k𝑘k-core percolation in the process of adding edges randomly to networks can be simply solved by deleting edges in the exact reverse order. In each step, if the edge to be deleted have already been removed in previous pruning process, then move to the next step. Otherwise, pruning process is carried out to find the new k𝑘k-core.

The relative size s𝑠s of the largest cluster after sample averaging is usually taken as the order parameter of percolation. Controlling parameter r𝑟r is defined as the edge number reduced by system size N𝑁N. For continuous percolation, s𝑠s along with the sizes of other ranked clusters changes continuously with r𝑟r and shows power-law decay with system size N𝑁N at the critical point rcsubscript𝑟𝑐r_{c}. Besides the sizes of ranked clusters themselves, percolation phase transitions can be characterized by the size jump behaviors of largest cluster in each realization of networksLee11 ; Nagler11 ; Qian12 ; Fan14 ; Zhu15a ; Zhu15b . V Summary

We have studied the k𝑘k-core percolation in ER networks through analysis of size jump behaviors of k𝑘k-core during the process of adding edge to networks. It’s confirmed that k𝑘k-core percolation is continuous when k=1,2𝑘12k=1,2 and discontinuous but with the characteristic of continuous phase transition when k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3. In other words, k𝑘k-core percolation is a hybrid first-order-second-order phase transition when k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3.

When k=1,2𝑘12k=1,2, the largest and second largest size jumps possess the same asymptotic properties as N→∞→𝑁N\to\infty. The 111-core percolation, namely percolation of giant component, belongs to the universality class of mean-field values of percolation in infinite dimension where ν=3𝜈3\nu=3 and β=1𝛽1\beta=1. The 222-core percolation belongs to a different universality class where ν=3𝜈3\nu=3 and β=2𝛽2\beta=2. There are some other percolation models sharing the same universality class, such as percolation of bi-connected component in undirected networks, percolation of giant strongly connected component, giant in- and out-component in directed networks.

When k≥3𝑘3k\geq 3, the asymptotic behaviors of the largest size jump reflect the discontinuity of k𝑘k-core percolation while the other smaller size jumps contain the properties of continuous percolation. Despite its hybrid nature, we obtain a set of exponents which are independent of k𝑘k.
